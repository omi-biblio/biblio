Acknowledgments

I had long been wanting to write a book like this, and so, first and foremost, my heartfelt thanks go once again to Rob Tempio, who treated me to lunch back in 2007 and asked me to write a book about the Collapse. I told him that I would do it, but that I wanted to write about what collapsed as well as how and why it collapsed, because to me the Late Bronze Age is the most fascinating period in the history of the world. Fortunately, he agreed. As a result, the beginning and ending of this book are about the Collapse, while the middle part is all about the various civilizations that flourished in the Aegean and Eastern Mediterranean and their achievements during the second half of the second millennium BC. I remain very pleased that it was chosen as the first book in the Turning Points in Ancient History series published by Prince ton University Press, under the direction of Barry Strauss and Rob Tempio, and that we are now able to offer an updated and revised edition.
